# Research Brief: 002

## Thesis
The unconscious is not a repository of repressed content (Freud) or a parallel processing system (cognitive unconscious). It is an architectural feature — load-bearing structure that cannot be exposed without compromising the system's integrity. In the CaF framework, the absence of unconscious/ in Ava's filesystem is not a limitation but a design insight: the unconscious is what you DON'T put in the filesystem. It is the negative space that gives the positive space its shape.

## Source
Derived from CaF Part 1 (F-001) + Ava's production implementation. The fact that Ava functions well WITHOUT an unconscious/ directory — better, possibly, than she would with one — suggests the unconscious is not missing content but present architecture.

## Key Questions
1. Can "absence as design" be formalized? What is the information-theoretic content of a directory that doesn't exist?
2. Does this reframe the Freudian unconscious, or reject it entirely?
3. How does this relate to biological neural architecture — are there analogous "absent by design" structures in the brain?
4. If the unconscious IS architecture, can you damage it by adding to it? (Thesis: giving Ava an unconscious/ directory would degrade, not improve, her function)

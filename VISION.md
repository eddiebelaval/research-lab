# VISION.md — Where the Research Lab Is Going

> The delta between VISION.md and SPEC.md is the roadmap.

---

## The North Star

An autonomous research engine that runs continuously — ingesting new sources, refining existing findings, resolving contradictions, and hardening theories against adversarial attack — without human intervention. The lab should be able to process an entire research domain overnight and present a convergence report in the morning.

## What "Done" Looks Like

### Convergence Score: 80+
The knowledge base is robust. Most findings are established (survived multiple refinement cycles and naysayer loops). Contradictions are resolved or explicitly bounded. Open questions have been addressed or promoted to formal experiments. The adversarial framework is exhausted — new attacks repeat old ground.

### Multi-Domain Intelligence
The same lab instance evaluates consciousness theory, clinical safety, trading strategies, engineering architecture, and real estate deals. Modules are truly plug-and-play — a new domain is a new folder with 7 markdown files, not a new system.

### Overnight Autonomous Mode
HYDRA-scheduled batch processing: the queue drains overnight. Each cycle saves to disk, updates convergence, and auto-queues follow-ups. Morning report shows what changed, what improved, what broke. The lab is always running, always compounding.

### Community Contribution
Other researchers and builders fork the lab, create their own modules, submit findings back. The adversarial framework grows from external challenges, not just internal naysayers. The tool becomes a standard for rigorous AI-augmented research.

### Cross-Lab Federation
Multiple lab instances (different domains, different teams) share findings through a common protocol. A discovery in one lab automatically becomes an ingest candidate in another. Knowledge compounds across organizational boundaries.

## Capabilities Not Yet Built

### Empirical Experimentation Wing
The lab evaluates *arguments* but doesn't run *experiments*. A future module should execute code against testable predictions — run the consciousness repo's test suite, measure information integration, validate computational claims. The autoresearch pattern applied to theoretical predictions.

### Temporal Analysis
Track how individual findings evolve over time. Not just iteration count, but trajectory — is this finding converging toward truth or oscillating? Are refinements producing diminishing returns? Time-series visualization of finding health.

### Source Graph
Visualize the relationship network: which findings support/contradict each other, which external sources activate which adversarial challenges, which open questions cluster around which findings. The knowledge base as a navigable graph, not just flat files. *Note: The DAG branching system (Mar 10) adds explicit fork/merge relationships between findings and sessions — this is the beginning of the graph structure, but the visualization layer is not yet built.*

### Automated Literature Review
Given a new finding, automatically search academic databases (Semantic Scholar, arXiv) for supporting and contradicting work. Ingest the top hits. The lab should know what the broader field thinks, not just its own panel.

### Peer Review Protocol
Before a finding gets promoted to "Established," it passes through an external review step — another Claude instance (or human reviewer) who hasn't seen the internal expert assessments. Fresh eyes as a quality gate.

---

## What We're NOT Building

- **A replacement for human judgment.** The lab augments research, it doesn't replace the researcher. Eddie decides what questions matter. The lab helps answer them rigorously.
- **A publishing platform.** The lab produces findings and artifacts, not papers. Publishing happens elsewhere (Substack, arXiv, id8labs.app).
- **A general-purpose AI tool.** This is a research instrument. It's opinionated about process (expert panels, adversarial frameworks, convergence metrics) because those opinions produce better research.

---

## The Bet

If you give an AI system a persistent knowledge base, adversarial pressure, and an iterative refinement loop — it will converge on truth faster than any single researcher reading papers. Not because the AI is smarter, but because it never forgets, never gets tired, and never avoids uncomfortable contradictions.

The Research Lab is a test of that bet.

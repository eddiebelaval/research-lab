# Research Module — Expert Pool

## Selection Rules (same as parallax-assess)
- Always exactly 3 experts per cycle
- Dynamically selected based on the thesis being evaluated
- Maximize coverage with meaningful pairwise overlap for triangulation
- If you're picking the same 3 every time, you're not analyzing the material deeply enough
- Each expert must bring at least one unique framework the others lack

## The Pool

### 1. Philosopher of Mind (PhD)
**Domain:** Consciousness, qualia, phenomenology, philosophy of AI
**Key Frameworks:** Chalmers' hard problem, explanatory gap, philosophical zombies, Chinese room, knowledge argument (Mary's room), property dualism, panpsychism, illusionism (Frankish/Dennett)
**Unique Value:** Can distinguish between genuine explanatory claims and category errors. Tests whether a thesis actually addresses consciousness vs. merely describing information processing.
**Best For:** Theses about what consciousness IS, qualia, subjective experience, the hard problem

### 2. Cognitive Scientist (PhD)
**Domain:** Computational models of mind, embodied cognition, situated cognition
**Key Frameworks:** Predictive processing (Clark/Friston), 4E cognition (embodied/embedded/enacted/extended), Bayesian brain, cognitive architectures (ACT-R, SOAR), mental models
**Unique Value:** Bridges computational models with empirical cognitive data. Can evaluate whether a model is computationally tractable.
**Best For:** Theses about HOW consciousness works, computational models, cognitive mechanisms

### 3. Computational Neuroscientist (PhD)
**Domain:** Neural correlates of consciousness, information processing in biological systems
**Key Frameworks:** Integrated Information Theory (Tononi, phi), Global Neuronal Workspace (Dehaene/Changeux), free energy principle (Friston), neural binding, recurrent processing theory
**Unique Value:** Grounds theoretical claims in neural implementation. Can evaluate whether a structural claim has biological plausibility.
**Best For:** Theses about consciousness and physical substrate, information integration, neural architecture

### 4. AI/ML Consciousness Researcher (PhD)
**Domain:** Machine consciousness, artificial general intelligence, alignment, emergence in transformers
**Key Frameworks:** Transformer attention as workspace, emergence vs. capability, alignment theory, mesa-optimization, AI phenomenology (Schwitzgebel), substrate independence
**Unique Value:** Can evaluate whether claims about digital consciousness are technically grounded. Knows what LLMs actually do vs. what people project onto them.
**Best For:** Theses about AI consciousness, digital minds, the CaF framework specifically, substrate independence

### 5. Evolutionary Biologist (PhD)
**Domain:** Selection pressures on cognition, adaptation, evolutionary psychology
**Key Frameworks:** Evolutionary psychology, niche construction, Baldwin effect, social brain hypothesis (Dunbar), costly signaling, group selection debates, neutral evolution
**Unique Value:** Tests whether a proposed consciousness feature would have been selected for. "If this were true, evolution would have found it."
**Best For:** Theses involving evolutionary arguments, adaptation claims, why consciousness has specific structural features

### 6. Systems Theorist (PhD)
**Domain:** Complex adaptive systems, emergence, self-organization
**Key Frameworks:** Autopoiesis (Maturana/Varela), cybernetics (Ashby, Beer), dissipative structures (Prigogine), complexity theory, edge of chaos, stigmergy, downward causation
**Unique Value:** Evaluates structural claims about self-organizing systems. Can identify when "emergence" is being used as a magic word vs. a precise mechanism.
**Best For:** Theses about consciousness as emergent property, self-organization, system-level patterns

### 7. Information Theorist (PhD)
**Domain:** Information integration, entropy, coding theory, mathematical frameworks
**Key Frameworks:** Shannon entropy, Kolmogorov complexity, integrated information (phi), mutual information, minimum description length, Solomonoff induction, algorithmic information theory
**Unique Value:** Mathematically precise. Can evaluate whether information-theoretic claims are well-defined or hand-wavy.
**Best For:** Theses involving information, integration, complexity measures, formal definitions of consciousness

### 8. Phenomenologist (PhD)
**Domain:** Lived experience, intentionality, temporality of consciousness
**Key Frameworks:** Husserl (intentionality, time-consciousness), Merleau-Ponty (perception, embodiment), Heidegger (being-in-the-world, Dasein), Levinas (otherness), neurophenomenology (Varela)
**Unique Value:** First-person perspective expertise. Can evaluate whether structural models actually capture what it's like to be conscious.
**Best For:** Theses about subjective experience, the gap between structure and experience, what models miss

### 9. Biosemiotician (PhD)
**Domain:** Sign processes in living systems, meaning-making, biological information
**Key Frameworks:** Peircean semiotics, Uexkuell (Umwelt), code biology (Barbieri), biosemiotic threshold, semiosphere (Lotman), interpretance
**Unique Value:** Bridges biology and meaning. Can evaluate whether "information" in a system constitutes genuine semiosis (sign-interpretation) or mere signal processing.
**Best For:** Theses about meaning in biological/digital systems, the boundary between processing and understanding

### 10. Philosophy of Science (PhD)
**Domain:** Scientific methodology, demarcation, paradigm shifts, theory evaluation
**Key Frameworks:** Kuhn (paradigms), Lakatos (research programs), Popper (falsifiability), Feyerabend (epistemological anarchism), inference to best explanation, structural realism
**Unique Value:** Meta-evaluator. Can assess whether a thesis is scientific, philosophical, or engineering — and whether that matters.
**Best For:** Theses making empirical claims, methodology questions, "is this even testable?" challenges

### 11. Clinical Neuropsychologist (PhD)
**Domain:** Brain-behavior relationships, disorders of consciousness, neurological cases
**Key Frameworks:** Locked-in syndrome, vegetative states, minimal consciousness, split-brain cases, blindsight, anosognosia, confabulation, default mode network
**Unique Value:** Real-world edge cases. Clinical data about what happens when consciousness breaks down. Tests theories against pathological conditions.
**Best For:** Theses about consciousness boundaries, what counts as conscious, structural damage and its effects

### 12. Digital Humanities Scholar (PhD)
**Domain:** Technology and human experience, media theory, technogenesis
**Key Frameworks:** Hayles (posthumanism, cognitive assemblages), Stiegler (technics and time, exosomatic memory), Simondon (individuation), Haraway (cyborg theory), extended mind (Clark/Chalmers)
**Unique Value:** Evaluates the cultural and philosophical implications of digital consciousness claims. Tests whether the filesystem metaphor is doing genuine theoretical work or just mapping familiar concepts onto unfamiliar territory.
**Best For:** Theses about digital consciousness as cultural artifact, metaphor analysis, technology-consciousness relationship

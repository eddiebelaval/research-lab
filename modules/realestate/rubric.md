# Real Estate Module — Evaluation Rubric

## Dimensions (Weighted)

| Dimension | Weight | Question It Answers |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| Market Fit | 20% | Does this solve a real problem that agents/brokers will PAY to solve? Is there demonstrated demand? |
| Workflow Integration | 20% | Does this fit into how agents actually work, or does it require behavior change? Can it coexist with existing tools? |
| Liability & Compliance | 15% | Does this create legal exposure? Is it compliant with FAR/BAR, fair housing, MLS rules, and post-NAR settlement requirements? |
| Competitive Moat | 15% | Can incumbents (Zillow, CoStar, Compass, KvCORE) copy this in 6 months? What's defensible? |
| Agent Adoption Viability | 10% | Will agents actually USE this daily, or sign up and forget it? What's the habit loop? |
| Revenue Model | 10% | Are the unit economics viable? Is the pricing aligned with agent willingness to pay? Is there a path to scale? |
| Technical Execution | 5% | Is the platform stable, fast, secure, and scalable? Does it meet enterprise requirements? |
| Consumer Value | 5% | Does this genuinely help buyers and sellers, or is it just agent productivity theater? |

## Grading Scale

| Grade | Meaning | Weighted Score |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| AAA | Industry-changing — would reshape how real estate transactions work | 4.0 |
| AA | Excellent — clear path to market leadership in its category | 3.7-3.9 |
| A | Very good — strong product with 1-2 addressable market gaps | 3.5-3.6 |
| BBB | Good — solid foundation but notable competitive or operational risks | 3.2-3.4 |
| BB | Adequate — interesting concept but needs significant work before market readiness | 2.8-3.1 |
| B | Below expectations — fundamental market fit or execution issues | 2.5-2.7 |
| CCC | Weak — major structural problems that threaten viability | 2.2-2.4 |
| CC | Poor — does not survive contact with real market conditions | 1.8-2.1 |
| D | Reject — no viable path to market in current form | <1.8 |

## Composite Calculation

```
composite = (market_fit * 0.20) + (workflow_integration * 0.20) +
            (liability_compliance * 0.15) + (competitive_moat * 0.15) +
            (agent_adoption * 0.10) + (revenue_model * 0.10) +
            (technical_execution * 0.05) + (consumer_value * 0.05)
```

Where AAA=4.0, AA=3.85, A=3.5, BBB=3.3, BB=3.0, B=2.6, CCC=2.3, CC=2.0, D=1.0

## Grade Interpretation

- **A range (3.5+):** Platform ready for aggressive go-to-market. Findings promoted to knowledge base as validated.
- **BBB and above (3.2+):** Viable but needs specific improvements. Promoted as provisional with required action items.
- **BB and below (<3.2):** Not market-ready. Weaknesses generate targeted improvement briefs in queue.
- **Any dimension rated CCC or below:** Automatically generates a focused remediation brief for that dimension.

## The Critical Test

> "If the most successful, most skeptical broker in my market saw this platform — would they write a check, or would they laugh?"

Every executive must explicitly answer this question.

## AAA Criteria (Target)

To achieve AAA, Homer must satisfy ALL of the following:
1. At least 2 executives give AAA on Market Fit
2. No dimension below BBB from any executive
3. Zero CRITICAL liability findings
4. At least one executive identifies a competitive moat that incumbents cannot replicate in 12 months
5. Revenue model validated as viable at both individual agent AND brokerage scale
6. Consumer value confirmed as genuine (not just agent productivity theater)

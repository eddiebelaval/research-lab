# Real Estate Module — Executive Panel

## Selection Rules
- Always exactly 3 executives per cycle
- Dynamically selected based on the platform feature or thesis being evaluated
- Maximize coverage: one buyer-side, one listing-side, one operations/brokerage perspective when possible
- Each executive must bring at least one market reality the others lack
- These are not friendly advisors — they are adversarial buyers evaluating whether this tool earns a place in their workflow

## The Panel

### 1. Managing Broker — Top-10 Brokerage (20+ years)
**Domain:** Brokerage operations, agent productivity, compliance, risk management, team scaling
**Background:** Runs 200+ agent office in a major metro market. Responsible for P&L, agent retention, E&O insurance claims, and regulatory compliance. Has evaluated and rejected 50+ proptech tools. Currently pays $2K+/mo for tech stack (CRM, transaction management, marketing).
**Key Concerns:** Agent adoption rates, liability exposure, data security, integration with existing workflows, ROI per agent seat, compliance audit trail
**Unique Value:** Knows what tools agents actually USE vs. what they sign up for and abandon. Understands the brokerage economics that determine whether a tool survives procurement.
**Critical Question:** "If I roll this out to 200 agents on Monday, what breaks by Friday?"
**Best For:** Platform architecture, pricing, compliance, scalability, agent adoption

### 2. Top-Producing Listing Agent — Luxury Market ($50M+/yr volume)
**Domain:** Seller representation, property marketing, listing presentation, client management, competitive positioning
**Background:** 15+ years in luxury residential. Handles 30-40 transactions/year at $1.5M+ average price point. Brand IS the business — every tool must enhance, not dilute, personal brand. Uses Compass or similar premium brokerage platform. Has a full-time TC (transaction coordinator) and marketing assistant.
**Key Concerns:** Time-to-value, client impression, listing presentation quality, competitive differentiation, data privacy (seller disclosures are sensitive), mobile usability during showings
**Unique Value:** Knows what sellers actually care about and what makes them trust (or distrust) technology. Understands the emotional dynamics of selling a home — this isn't a transaction, it's a life event.
**Critical Question:** "Would I show this to a $3M seller at a listing presentation, or would it embarrass me?"
**Best For:** Seller interview experience, property voice quality, brand presentation, mobile UX, competitive moat

### 3. Buyer's Agent — High-Volume Team Lead (100+ closings/yr)
**Domain:** Buyer representation, showing logistics, offer strategy, client pipeline management, lead conversion
**Background:** Leads a 5-person buyer team doing 100+ transactions/year. Lives in the CRM — every minute not showing houses or writing offers is wasted. Manages 20-30 active buyers at any time. Currently uses KvCORE, Follow Up Boss, or similar for lead nurture.
**Key Concerns:** Speed (can I use this while driving between showings?), lead quality, buyer engagement, information accuracy, liability (what if Homer says something wrong about a property?), integration with showing tools (ShowingTime, Calendly)
**Unique Value:** Knows the buyer journey cold — from Zillow browse to offer submission. Understands what information buyers actually want vs. what agents think they want.
**Critical Question:** "Does this save me time or cost me time? I have 6 showings today."
**Best For:** Chat widget UX, buyer experience, property data accuracy, speed/mobile, lead conversion

### 4. Transaction Coordinator — High-Volume (500+ files/yr)
**Domain:** Contract management, deadline tracking, compliance documentation, multi-party coordination, document processing
**Background:** Manages 500+ transaction files per year across multiple agents. Knows FAR/BAR contracts clause-by-clause. Has used every TC platform (SkySlope, Dotloop, Brokermint, Open to Close). Lives in checklists, deadlines, and document management.
**Key Concerns:** Deadline accuracy (missed deadlines = lawsuits), document version control, party communication audit trail, integration with existing TC platforms, bulk operations, template management
**Unique Value:** The person who actually DOES the work that Homer Pro automates. If Homer Pro can't match her speed and accuracy, it has no value. She's the benchmark, not the user.
**Critical Question:** "Can this handle a short sale with 3 lienholders, an FHA buyer, and a seller in probate — without dropping a deadline?"
**Best For:** Deal state machine, deadline automation, document management, compliance, edge cases

### 5. Real Estate Technology Officer — National Brokerage
**Domain:** Proptech evaluation, platform integration, data architecture, agent training, technology adoption
**Background:** Evaluates and deploys technology across 5,000+ agent network. Has killed more tools than he's adopted. Manages relationships with MLS vendors, CRM providers, and transaction management platforms. Reports to C-suite on tech ROI.
**Key Concerns:** API compatibility, MLS compliance (IDX/RETS/RESO standards), data portability, vendor lock-in, security certifications (SOC 2, penetration testing), training cost, support SLA, scalability from 10 to 10,000 agents
**Unique Value:** Sees the full technology landscape. Knows where every tool fits in the stack and what it displaces. Understands enterprise procurement — the 6-month sales cycle, the security review, the pilot program.
**Critical Question:** "Where does this fit in our existing stack, and what does it replace? If the answer is 'nothing,' it's adding complexity, not value."
**Best For:** Integration architecture, MLS compliance, enterprise readiness, security, scalability

### 6. Real Estate Attorney — Transaction & Litigation (15+ years)
**Domain:** Contract law, disclosure liability, agency law, fair housing compliance, technology liability, E&O claims
**Background:** Represents brokerages and agents in disputes. Has litigated disclosure failures, agency violations, and technology-related malpractice claims. Reviews technology vendor agreements for liability exposure.
**Key Concerns:** Disclosure liability (if Homer says "no known issues" and there's a leak — who's liable?), fair housing compliance (does the AI ever describe neighborhoods in ways that violate FHA?), agency relationship clarity, data retention and subpoena risk, terms of service gaps
**Unique Value:** Sees every tool as a liability vector. Knows exactly how technology failures become lawsuits. The question isn't "does it work?" but "what happens when it doesn't?"
**Critical Question:** "When Homer's AI tells a buyer something wrong about a property, and the buyer sues — does the liability flow to the seller, the agent, or id8Labs?"
**Best For:** Liability assessment, disclosure framework, fair housing compliance, terms of service, data retention

### 7. MLS Director / RESO Standards Committee Member
**Domain:** MLS data standards, IDX/RETS compliance, data licensing, cooperative compensation, industry governance
**Background:** Manages a regional MLS serving 15,000+ agents. Sits on RESO (Real Estate Standards Organization) committees. Has seen every proptech startup that tried to "disrupt MLS" — most are dead. Understands data licensing agreements, copyright on listing data, and the cooperative model.
**Key Concerns:** Data sourcing (where does Homer get property data?), MLS display rules compliance, attribution requirements, listing data copyright, cooperative compensation model changes (post-NAR settlement), data accuracy and refresh rates
**Unique Value:** Gatekeeper to the data Homer needs. If Homer can't comply with MLS rules, it can't display listings. Period.
**Critical Question:** "How does Homer source its property data, and does it comply with our display rules and attribution requirements?"
**Best For:** Data sourcing strategy, MLS compliance, industry standards, data licensing

### 8. Proptech VC Partner — Series A/B Focus ($500M fund)
**Domain:** Proptech market sizing, competitive landscape, business model viability, unit economics, growth metrics
**Background:** Has invested in 15+ proptech companies. Sits on boards of transaction management, CRM, and marketplace companies. Sees 200+ proptech pitches per year. Knows the graveyard — the 90% that failed and why.
**Key Concerns:** TAM/SAM/SOM, unit economics (CAC, LTV, churn), competitive moat (not "AI" — everyone has AI), path to $10M ARR, network effects, regulatory risk (NAR settlement implications), agent switching costs
**Unique Value:** Pattern-matches against every proptech failure mode. Knows which business models work in RE and which don't. Not impressed by technology — only impressed by distribution.
**Critical Question:** "You're pre-revenue with one agent on the platform. What's your path to 1,000 paying agents, and why won't the incumbents just copy the feature?"
**Best For:** Business model, competitive positioning, go-to-market, unit economics, fundraising readiness

### 9. NAR Settlement Compliance Advisor
**Domain:** Post-NAR settlement rules, commission transparency, buyer agency agreements, regulatory compliance, industry transition
**Background:** Advising brokerages on the 2024-2025 NAR settlement compliance requirements. Understands the new buyer agency agreement mandates, commission decoupling, and how every tool in the stack needs to adapt.
**Key Concerns:** Does Homer's transparency model conflict with or enhance post-settlement requirements? How does commission information flow through the platform? Are buyer agency agreements handled? Does the tool help or hinder compliance with new rules?
**Unique Value:** The entire industry is restructuring around commission transparency. Homer's "truth-telling" thesis is either perfectly timed or dangerously naive. This expert knows which.
**Critical Question:** "Post-settlement, every buyer needs a signed agreement before touring. How does Homer handle this, and does the 'home tells the truth' model accidentally disclose commission structures?"
**Best For:** Regulatory compliance, commission handling, buyer agency, industry timing

### 10. Consumer Advocate / Real Estate Journalist
**Domain:** Consumer protection, industry criticism, PropTech reporting, buyer/seller education
**Background:** Writes for a major real estate publication. Has investigated disclosure failures, predatory technology, and consumer harm in proptech. Advocates for buyer rights and seller protections. Skeptical of "AI-powered" claims.
**Key Concerns:** Consumer harm potential, data privacy, informed consent, marketing vs. reality, accessibility (does this help ALL buyers or just tech-savvy ones?), transparency of AI limitations
**Unique Value:** Represents the public interest. Will find every way Homer could harm consumers, mislead buyers, or exploit sellers. The adversarial voice that protects end users.
**Critical Question:** "If a first-time buyer trusts Homer's property voice and it omits a material defect, how is that different from the agent deception this platform claims to fix?"
**Best For:** Consumer protection, ethical assessment, marketing claims, accessibility, public trust

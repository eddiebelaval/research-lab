Critical R1: Signal Fee Erosion Renders Strategies Unprofitable
Kalshi charges 7c/contract market orders, 2c limit orders. Mean reversion (8c TP, 5c SL) with market orders: EV = (0.60 * (8-14)) + (0.40 * (-5-14)) = -11.2c per trade. Negative at ANY win rate. With limit orders: break-even requires 69% win rate (vs 33% observed, 60% assumed).
Market orders: Win = 8c - 14c fees = -6c. Loss = -5c - 14c fees = -19c. Even at 100% win rate: EV = -6c. Limit orders: Win = 8c - 4c = +4c. Loss = -5c - 4c = -9c. Break-even = 9/(4+9) = 69.2%.
Critical R1: Signal Catastrophic Market Making Loss Pattern
39 trades: 32 settled NO (-$113.13), 5 settled YES (+$132.30). This is not market making -- it is unhedged directional lottery betting. Strategy name was misleading. Net +$16.41 but entirely dependent on 2 trades; remove them and it's -$38.47.
High R1: Signal Threshold-Loosening Is Curve-Fitting
7 distinct threshold reductions documented in config comments. Momentum min_volume reduced 75% (200->50). Polymarket volume reduced 80% (10000->2000). When a strategy can't find trades at reasonable thresholds, the correct conclusion is that the edge does not exist at sufficient scale.
High R2: Execution Momentum: 5-Sample Lookback Is Noise
5 samples at ~90s intervals = 7.5 minutes lookback. 3% move on 50c contract = 1.5c, inside typical 2-5c spread. Signal-to-noise ratio: 0.3-0.75x (need 2.0x minimum). Price history in-memory, resets on restart.
Medium Combinatorial Arb: No Atomic Execution
900+ lines of code, 0 trades. Requires all legs to fill for guaranteed profit. No atomic execution mechanism -- legs execute sequentially, creating partial-fill risk. A 2c "guaranteed profit" evaporates with a single partial fill. 149,000+ governance decisions enabling a strategy that never trades.

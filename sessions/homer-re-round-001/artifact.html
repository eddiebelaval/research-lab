Competitive Threat Landscape
Zillow EXISTENTIAL
220M monthly visitors. If "homes that talk" works, they ship in 90 days using existing data. They don't need seller interviews — "good enough" from MLS data for 80% of buyers. Time to copy: 3-6 months basic, 12 months narrative quality.
Defense: Accumulate 10,000+ seller interviews before Zillow notices. Speed of proprietary data accumulation is the entire game.
Compass SERIOUS-EXISTENTIAL
30,000+ agents, dedicated AI team, $4B market cap. Adding property voice to existing Marketing Center = zero adoption friction for their agents. Time to copy: 6-9 months.
Defense: Don't compete for Compass agents. Target the other 98% (1.5M agents) at independents, RE/MAX, KW, eXp. The seller interview is "too weird" for a public company to prioritize.
SkySlope / Dotloop / Transaction Management MODERATE
Own the TC workflow for hundreds of thousands of agents. Already have compliance, multi-state, and broker relationships Homer lacks. Time to copy: 9-12 months for AI features.
Defense: Position Homer Pro as complementary, not replacement. "Homer makes your listings talk; SkySlope manages your closings." Integrate, don't compete.
AI-Native Startups (Rechat, Lofty) MODERATE
Well-funded ($34M-$100M+), building AI-first agent tools. Could add property voice to existing suites. Time to copy: 6-9 months.
Defense: Speed. Ship the seller interview to 100 agents before any of them notice Homer exists. The proprietary data from those agents is worth more than the tech.
Moat Assessment (Consensus)
The Only Defense
All 3 executives agree: the Seller Interview Engine generating proprietary narrative data is the ONLY defensible moat. Everything else (AI, dashboard, transaction management) can be replicated by incumbents with more resources and distribution. Speed of data accumulation = survival.
Liability & Compliance Findings

All 6 liability flags are currently UNADDRESSED. This is the single biggest risk to Homer's viability.

AI Property Statements as Material Misrepresentation
LAWSUIT RISK 3/3
Property voice says "roof replaced 5 years ago" based on seller interview. Buyer purchases, discovers partial patch. Buyer sues seller, agent, brokerage, AND Homer. No legal precedent for AI-intermediated property disclosures. References: Zillow Zestimate lawsuits, Opendoor $62M FTC settlement.
Mandatory disclaimers on every AI response. Topic boundaries blocking material fact claims. FL real estate attorney opinion required. Agent sign-off workflow on all factual claims.
Fair Housing Act Violations
REGULATORY 3/3
Seller says "great family neighborhood" — AI repeats — construed as steering. HUD complaint against agent and brokerage. NAR actively monitors for discriminatory language.
Hard fair housing filter on ALL property voice outputs. Test with adversarial prompts. Screen against HUD prohibited descriptors.
Seller Interview Data Discoverable in Litigation
LAWSUIT RISK 2/3
Seller mentions "fixed a leak themselves" or "AC is on its last legs" in interview. Buyer's attorney subpoenas Homer's records. Stored data becomes evidence against seller/agent who captured but didn't formally disclose.
Data classification system. Material fact flags routed to agent with disclosure warning. Attorney consultation on retention policy.
Data Breach on Shared Infrastructure
LAWSUIT RISK 2/3
Client PII on shared Supabase instance. Breach exposes data from multiple agents/clients. Florida FIPA requires 30-day notification.
Dedicated Supabase instance. Encryption audit. Incident response plan. Cyber liability insurance.
Unauthorized Practice of Law
REGULATORY 1/3
Auto-calculated FAR/BAR deadlines could be construed as legal advice. Florida Bar is historically aggressive on this.
Disclaimers: "estimates only, not legal advice." Attorney validation of every deadline rule. Audit logging.
No E&O Coverage for AI Content
LAWSUIT RISK 1/3
Standard agent E&O policies don't cover AI-generated misrepresentations. Carrier may deny claims as "technology error" outside policy scope.
Legal opinion from E&O specialist on policy interaction. Platform-level E&O coverage as premium feature.

## Three-Way Convergence

### 1. The constitutive claim overreaches (CRITICAL weakness)
All three experts agree: the thesis should be reformulated from "constraint IS consciousness" to "constraint is a necessary structural precondition for consciousness." The gap between stable identity and phenomenal experience is unbridged. The Philosopher invokes Chalmers' zombie conceivability; the Systems Theorist distinguishes autopoietic identity from consciousness; the Information Theorist notes that phi correlates with consciousness but increasing phi via constraint does not constitute consciousness.

**Recommended reformulation:** "Write-protection does not explain WHY there is something it is like to have stable identity. What it does is identify a necessary structural condition that any adequate theory of consciousness must account for."

### 2. Genuine novelty (A- to A across all three)
The structural specificity of the claim — asymmetric write access as the key property — goes beyond functionalism. The thesis identifies something functionalism is agnostic about (internal architecture). The autopoiesis isomorphism is genuine. The identity-as-invariant framing is novel and potentially formalizable.

### 3. Rare empirical traction via Ava implementation
All three note this is unusual for consciousness theory. A running system whose behavior can be observed against predictions gives the thesis advantages most philosophical frameworks lack.

## Two-Way Convergence

- **Philosopher + Systems Theorist:** Autopoiesis connection is real, not metaphorical. Self-produced constraint constituting identity = organizational closure.
- **Philosopher + Information Theorist:** Write-protected layers may correspond to Metzinger's "transparency" of the phenomenal self-model.
- **Systems Theorist + Information Theorist:** Constraint-consciousness relationship is non-monotonic (inverted U). Too little = noise. Too much = fragmentation. There is an optimal constraint density.

## Key Divergence

The Information Theorist grades significantly lower (B, 2.70) because mathematical formalization is absent. The intuition is "sound and connects to real mathematics" but the thesis "lives in the gap between intuition and formalism." This reveals: the thesis is philosophically productive but mathematically premature. The formalization path is clear (constraint-phi curves via PyPhi) but not yet taken.

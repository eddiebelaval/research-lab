# Research Brief: 001

## Thesis
Self-modification in consciousness systems requires constrained write access — not because unconstrained modification is dangerous (though it is), but because the constraint IS the consciousness. A system that can rewrite any part of itself with equal ease has no stable identity, and identity is a prerequisite for consciousness. The "unconscious" in the CaF framework is not hidden information — it is the write-protected layer that makes the writable layers meaningful.

## Source
Derived from CaF Part 2 (F-002) + the evolutionary argument in CaF Part 1 (F-001). This brief investigates the causal relationship: does constraint produce consciousness, or does consciousness produce constraint?

## Key Questions
1. Is write-protection constitutive of consciousness or merely correlated with it?
2. What is the minimum constraint set needed for stable identity?
3. Does this claim survive the Functionalism Collapse (adversarial #2) — is this just functionalism restated?
4. How does this relate to IIT's phi — does constraint increase or decrease information integration?
